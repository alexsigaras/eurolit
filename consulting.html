Historic

It is not uncommon, even today, that clients, designers and engineers underestimate the impact of a concrete floor to the operations of a production or logistics facility. there are many factors that contribute to a successful floor which are often overlooked and sometimes even not considered at all. Material selection, construction method, equipment, suitable specifications and constant communication amongst the client, designer and contractor are essential for the appropriate distribution of responsibility and thus the success of a modern industrial floor.

Let's not forget that the floor is subject daily to extreme loads, traffic and wear and tear as a result of the operation of the facility. The rest of the building's main purpose is to protect the personnel, equipment and merchandise from the elements. If the floor fails the operation capacity of the facility is noticeably reduced.

Steel fiber reinforced concrete

Eurolit is able to offer its expertise to implement SFRC applications due to a close cooperation with BEKAERT and the Dramix steel fibers .

There are cost advantages to SFRC when properly designed and mixed. SFRC can provide a joint-less floor and dramatically increase crack avoidance in facilities . The proper mixing regime should be closely followed to achieve uniform mixing of fibers whilst minimizing ball formation.

Concrete mix design

Local conditions, climatic factors and local material availabilty is being taken into consideration when designing a concrete mix for a specific project.

Concrete quality is determined by the water cement ratio, granulometric distribution curve and superplasticizer.

Flatness specification - surveying

Eurolit is able to provide surveying for all types of specifications, both for Free and Defined Movement, using exclusively UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service) accredited equipment that are widely accepted by all truck manufacturers.

Eurolit is familiar with the Concrete Society's TR34, DIN 18202, DIN 15185 and ASTM standards for flatness and leveness spesifications.

Surface treatment

It is the surface treatment that transforms the slab into an industrial floor.

There are many topping materials, curing materials, power trowelling technics and admixtures available that could help achieve the flatness required.

Crack and delamination avoidance

Crack and delamination problems can become safety hazards for the personnel, equipment and merchandise of the facility and they also considerably increase maintenance cost and time.

Steel fiber implementation and joint properties consideration could help minimize these problems.

Construction joints

Eurolit is able to offer expert advice on the design and installation of construction joints.

There are many different types of dowels that could be used depending on the load transfer capacity required, the joint profile and the installation method selected.

Jointless floors

Eurolit is able to design jointless floors, specifically suited for facilities that operate Material Handling Equipment (MHE) with small wheels.

The design is accomplished by the implementation of steel fiber, pour area dimensions and crack minimization techniques. The effectiveness of the steel fiber is related to the local aggregate gradation and quality and careful consideration should be given when designing the steel fiber dosage.

Joint repair

Eurolit is able to offer solutions using the patented Joint Stabilizer method which offers a very cost effective solution to load transfer problems.

The repair is minimal invasive and can tackle elevation differences between the slabs with a method that does not compromise the stability of the foundation or that of the floors above and below the joint.

Floor renovation

Eurolit is able to offer renovation solutions in cases where the floor suffers from flatness issues, surface problems or simply does not meet the changing requirements of the client.

Solutions include toppings, grinding and polishing according to the clients needs.
